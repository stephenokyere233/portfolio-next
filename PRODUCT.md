# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, technical leads, and potential collaborators scanning developer portfolios to assess competence and taste. They're evaluating quickly, usually on a laptop during a workday. They want to see real work, confirm technical depth, and gauge professionalism in under 60 seconds.

## Product Purpose

Personal portfolio for Stephen Okyere, a software developer. Showcases shipped projects across web and mobile, communicates technical capability, and provides a direct path to contact. Success is a visitor who leaves thinking "this person builds serious things."

## Brand Personality

Confident, clean, sharp. No fluff. The work speaks; the design frames it without competing. Technical competence expressed through restraint, not decoration.

## Anti-references

- Over-designed agency sites with excessive motion, parallax layers, and animated everything. Theatrics signal insecurity, not skill.
- Generic dark-mode developer portfolios with purple/blue gradients, Space Grotesk, and gradient text. The 2023 template look.
- SaaS landing page templates with metric cards, testimonial grids, and feature comparison tables.

## Design Principles

1. **The work is the product.** Every design decision serves the portfolio pieces. The frame never outshines the art.
2. **Precision over decoration.** Confidence comes from what you don't add. Every element earns its pixel.
3. **Respect the visitor's time.** Clear hierarchy, scannable structure, no friction between landing and contacting.
4. **Show, don't tell.** A well-built portfolio IS the proof of competence. The design itself is a portfolio piece.
5. **Quiet confidence.** No exclamation marks, no "stunning and intuitive," no self-promotion that tries too hard. Let quality do the convincing.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA. High contrast ratios on light backgrounds. Keyboard navigable. Reduced-motion support for all transitions. Semantic HTML throughout.
